# Example: Closer Call — Gym Owner (C+ Grade)

This example shows a complete Sales Call Review run for a closer call where the deal almost closed but stalled to "let me talk to my wife and circle back." The closer (Mackie) did a lot right, but several back-half leaks turned a probable close into a pending follow-up. This is the kind of call most owners need to dissect in a 1:1 because it shows up the most often — the deal that *almost* happens.

Missed Moment 1 — The Price Reveal Was Naked (5:33)

What was missed: You revealed the price with no anchor before, no

Marcus did the math himself and arrived at the largest possible 
number first ($10,800 + ad spend = $22-25K). His brain went to

The right sequence:

(a) Anchor before reveal. Right before "investment-wise...":
"So before I give you the number — I want you to know our typical 
gym recovers their full 6-month investment within 90 days from 
the first challenge. We've had gyms recover it in 45 days. So

this' — it's 'can we afford to wait another 90 days at 145 
members.'"

(b) Reveal the number AND the value at the same time:
"$1,800 a month for 6 months. We typically deliver a 2-3x return 
on that within the first 90 days based on our averages."

(c) Trial close immediately after:
"How does that land?"

That sequence prevents the prospect from doing the multiplication 
in their head before they've absorbed the value. You let Marcus 
do the multiplication. He's been thinking about $10,800 for the 
last six minutes of the call.

him a graceful exit ("walk away friends"), and asks for a 
specific yes/no on a specific time. He can say no to that — but 
the no will be a real no, not a stall.
